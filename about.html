Profile

Decades spanning excellence

Service to Society

Global Recognition

Professor Chandru was elected a Technology Pioneer of the World Economic Forum in 2006 and served on the Forum's global agenda council on the future of the health sector for 2014–2016. Biospectrum Magazine named him the Biotech Entrepreneur of 2007 in India.

Industry Leadership

The Association of Biotech led Enterprises (ABLE) elected Chandru as Honorary President for a three-year term (2009–2012). He currently serves on the State Vision Groups for Science & Technology as well as Biotechnology.

Policy & Advisory

As an author of the Atal Innovation Mission report (August 2015), he served on the mission high-level committee convened by the Prime Minister's Office at NITI Aayog.

Digital Humanities

Since 2009, Chandru has been actively engaged in digital humanities as a founder trustee of the International Institute for Art, Culture and Democracy (IIACD).

Mentorship

Chandru has mentored numerous entrepreneurs and early-stage ventures in education, technology, and life sciences.

Scientific Pursuit

The journey into life and health science for Professor Chandru, which began in the mid-90s, has been a story of science meets technology that leads to innovation — the way it is supposed to work.

The Genomics Revolution

Molecular biology got a great boost in the late 80s when the Human Genome Project was conceived, with the US and UK governments investing around $3 billion over 15 years to sequence and verify the human reference genome.

Applied Biosystems Agilent Affymetrix Thermo Fisher BioRad Qiagen Roche

The 4th paradigm of science had arrived with a bang in biology, and the community of biologists was unprepared. Strand appeared on the scene early in this phase.

Software & Research Partnerships

While Strand partnered with Agilent on the GeneSpring® platform, research collaborations flourished. Strand's scientists were co-authors on numerous high-impact publications, including two articles in Cell that made the cover in 2010.

Ganit Labs & Public-Private Partnership

In 2010, Strand ventured into setting up the first next-generation sequencing lab in Bangalore through Ganit Labs. Ganit Labs successfully assembled the genome of the Neem Tree (Azadirachta indica).
